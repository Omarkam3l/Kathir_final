## 7. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS

### Primary Segments

**1. Large Restaurant Chains (Priority Segment)**
- **Profile**: 10+ locations, high waste volume
- **Size**: 500+ chains in Egypt
- **Waste Volume**: 2-5 tons/location/month
- **Pain Points**: High disposal costs, brand reputation
- **Value Drivers**: Cost savings, sustainability reporting
- **Examples**: McDonald's, KFC, local chains

**2. Food Manufacturers**
- **Profile**: Production facilities with consistent waste
- **Size**: 1,200+ facilities in Egypt
- **Waste Volume**: 10-100 tons/facility/month
- **Pain Points**: Waste disposal costs, compliance
- **Value Drivers**: Revenue generation, efficiency
- **Examples**: Bakeries, dairy, meat processors

**3. Supermarket Chains**
- **Profile**: Large retail stores with mixed waste
- **Size**: 300+ major stores
- **Waste Volume**: 5-15 tons/store/month
- **Pain Points**: Perishable waste, ESG goals
- **Value Drivers**: Cost reduction, brand image
- **Examples**: Carrefour, Metro, local chains

**4. Hotels & Catering Companies**
- **Profile**: Large hospitality operations
- **Size**: 800+ large hotels
- **Waste Volume**: 3-10 tons/hotel/month
- **Pain Points**: Buffet waste, sustainability pressure
- **Value Drivers**: Cost savings, green certifications
- **Examples**: Marriott, Hilton, event caterers

**5. Food Distribution Centers**
- **Profile**: Wholesale and distribution warehouses
- **Size**: 150+ major centers
- **Waste Volume**: 20-50 tons/center/month
- **Pain Points**: Damaged goods, expired inventory
- **Value Drivers**: Revenue recovery, efficiency
- **Examples**: Wholesalers, importers, distributors

### Secondary Segments (Processors)

**1. Biogas/Bioenergy Plants**
- **Need**: High-moisture organic waste
- **Volume**: 1,000-5,000 tons/month
- **Value**: Renewable energy production

**2. Animal Feed Manufacturers**
- **Need**: Bakery waste, grains, produce
- **Volume**: 500-2,000 tons/month
- **Value**: Cost-effective feed ingredients

**3. Composting Facilities**
- **Need**: Fruit/vegetable waste, organics
- **Volume**: 300-1,500 tons/month
- **Value**: Quality compost production

**4. Specialized Processors**
- **Need**: Specific waste types (pharma, cosmetics, etc.)
- **Volume**: 20-200 tons/month
- **Value**: High-value product inputs

**5. Waste Management Companies**
- **Need**: General waste for sorting/processing
- **Volume**: 1,000-5,000 tons/month
- **Value**: Feedstock for operations

### Segment Prioritization

**Phase 1 (Year 1):**
- Focus: Large restaurant chains and food manufacturers
- Reason: High volume, consistent waste, strong ROI

**Phase 2 (Year 2):**
- Add: Supermarkets and hotels
- Reason: Expand waste diversity, increase volume

**Phase 3 (Year 3):**
- Add: Distribution centers and smaller businesses
- Reason: Market saturation, long-tail growth

## 9. REVENUE STREAMS

### Primary Revenue Streams

**1. Transaction Fees (60% of total revenue)**

**Structure:**
- Commission on waste transactions: 8-12%
- Tiered based on volume:
  - Small (<10 tons): 12% commission
  - Medium (10-50 tons): 10% commission
  - Large (>50 tons): 8% commission

**Pricing Logic:**
- Average transaction: $800 (20 tons × $40/ton)
- Average commission: $80 per transaction
- Charged to waste generator (seller)
- Automatic deduction from payment

**Revenue Potential:**
- Year 1: $48K (600 transactions)
- Year 2: $750K (9,000 transactions)
- Year 3: $3.2M (40,000 transactions)
